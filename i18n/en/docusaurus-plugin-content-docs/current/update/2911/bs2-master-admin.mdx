The <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> feature is designed to **enhance device security** by **strengthening overall administrator privileges** and **preventing unauthorized access and configuration changes**.
With this feature, you **must register a Master Admin** on the new device, and only registered Master Admins can access the administrator menu and change device settings.

The <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> feature is available **only on Suprema products that support user interface via LCD screen**.

:::info

* This feature is an essential policy to enhance device security.

* The <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> cannot be deleted directly, and they are **deleted only through factory reset**.

:::

### Registering Master Admin in BioStar 2

When you boot a new device for the first time after connecting it, the <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> registration screen appears. The new device must register a <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" />, and if not registered, the use of the device will be restricted.

In BioStar 2, you can register and manage <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" />.

:::caution

- If only new devices without a <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> registered are connected to BioStar 2, you cannot register or manage the <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> in BioStar 2.

- Devices without a <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> registered have limited features, such as credential scanning (for example, face, fingerprint, or card input). To enroll the credentials, use a device with a <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> already registered, or use a device that does not support the <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> feature.

:::

:::info

* The <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> feature is supported from **BioStar 2 v2.9.11** onwards.

* For existing devices with upgraded firmware, the <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> menu does not appear in BioStar 2 device settings.<br/>Existing devices upgraded via firmware do not provide <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> settings, but you can enhance administrator security by enabling the <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> option. For <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> settings, refer to the [following](#2step-authentication).

:::

1. To register <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> in BioStar 2, go to the <Cmd sid="common.device" /> menu, click the desired device, and enter the device detail page.

1. In the <Cmd sid="device.info.advanced" /> → <Cmd sid="device.info.administrator" /> → <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> menu, select and enroll two types of desired credentials.

### Managing Master Admin in BioStar 2

Registered <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> can be managed in BioStar 2. To manage <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" />, follow these steps:

1. Go to the <Cmd sid="common.device" /> menu, click the desired device, and enter the device detail page.

1. <Cmd sid="device.info.advanced" /> → <Cmd sid="device.info.administrator" /> → <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> menu, you can view enrolled credentials and add, edit, or delete them.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-master-admin-2.png' />

1. Click the <Cmd sid="button.apply" /> button to apply your changes.


### Batch Editing Master Admin

You can batch edit <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> credentials. Use the <Cmd sid="button.batchEdit" /> feature to enroll or modify master administrator credentials for multiple devices simultaneously.


1. Go to the <Cmd sid="common.device" /> menu, <Image src='/img/common/ico-checkbox.png' ico alone /> check the devices you want to batch edit in the left checkboxes, then click <Image src='/img/ico-batch-edit.png' ico /> at the top.

1. Click <Image src='/img/common/ico-batch-edit-activate-button.png' ico alone /> for <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> to switch to edit mode, then you can enroll, edit, or delete the desired credentials.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-batch-edit-master-admin.png' width='600px' />

    :::info

    You can enroll face, fingerprint, card, and PIN credentials, and at least two different types of credentials must be enrolled.

    :::

1. Click the <Cmd sid="button.apply" /> button to apply your changes.

    :::info

    * Batch editing overwrites with the enrolled credentials.

    * If the selected device is not a new device or does not support the configured credentials, the settings will not be applied to that device.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-batch-edit-master-admin-2.png' />

    :::

## Two-step Authentication {#2step-authentication}

Existing devices upgraded via firmware do not provide <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.title" /> settings, but you can enhance administrator security by enabling the <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> option.

:::info

* The <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> option appears only when the firmware has been upgraded to the latest version on existing devices.

* The default value for <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> is single-step authentication.

* <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> can be activated only if all registered administrators have at least two types of credentials.

* The firmware upgrade device cannot be downgraded to a lower version after upgrading the firmware.

:::

### Setting Up Two-Step Authentication in BioStar 2

1. To setting <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> in BioStar 2, go to the <Cmd sid="common.device" /> menu, click the desired device, and enter the device detail page.

    :::caution

    If no full administrator is configured on the device, the following popup message appears. Add an administrator to <Cmd sid="device.operatorLevel.all" /> in the <Cmd sid="device.info.advanced" /> → <Cmd sid="device.info.administrator" /> tab.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-admin-warning.png' />

    :::

1. Activate <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> by setting it to <Cmd sid="common.use" /> in <Cmd sid="device.info.advanced" /> → <Cmd sid="device.info.administrator" />.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-2step-auth-1.png' />

1. Click the <Cmd sid="button.apply" /> button to activate <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" />.

    :::info

    If not all administrators have at least two types of credentials, activation will fail and an error message will appear.<br/>Enroll at least two types of credentials for all administrators, and then try again.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-2step-auth-warning.png' />

    :::

    :::warning

    If you activate <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> and then delete credentials so that all administrators have fewer than two types, you will not be able to access the administrator menu if BioStar 2 connection is also unavailable. Therefore, exercise caution when deleting administrator credentials.

    :::

### Batch Editing Two-Step Authentication

You can batch edit <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" />. Use the <Cmd sid="button.batchEdit" /> feature to enable or disable <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> for multiple devices simultaneously.

1. Go to the <Cmd sid="common.device" /> menu, <Image src='/img/common/ico-checkbox.png' ico alone /> check the devices you want to batch edit in the left checkboxes, then click <Image src='/img/ico-batch-edit.png' ico /> at the top.

1. Click <Image src='/img/common/ico-batch-edit-activate-button.png' ico alone /> for <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" /> to switch to edit mode, then you can enable or disable <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" />.

    <Image src='/img/biostar2-batch-edit-2step-auth.png' width='600px' />

    :::caution

    When enabling <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" />, administrators with insufficient enrolled credentials may be unable to authenticate. Therefore, before enabling <Cmd sid="device.masterAdmin.twoStepAuth.title" />, ensure that **all administrators have at least two types of credentials enrolled**.

    :::

1. Click the <Cmd sid="button.apply" /> button to apply your changes.

## Additional Information

* **Manual Device Hash Key Change**

  * When manually changing the device hash key, a warning message will indicate that Master Admin PINs will be deleted. Please confirm the message before proceeding.

* **RS-485 Biometric Image Transmission Restriction**

  * Biometric credential images are not transmitted over RS-485 communication, so substitute images appear when viewing the registered Master Admin face on slave devices.
